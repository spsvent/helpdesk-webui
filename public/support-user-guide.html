STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

SkyPark Help Desk System

Accessing the Help Desk

Preferred Access Method: The Help Desk is available as an app in Microsoft Teams. Look for the Help Desk icon in your Teams sidebar. This is the recommended way to access the system.

Alternative access: https://tickets.spsvent.net

Ticket Creation Authorization

Important: Having a SkyPark email address does NOT authorize you to create tickets.

Who May Create Tickets:

  • Managers and Supervisors are authorized to create tickets
  • Managers/Supervisors are responsible for creating tickets on behalf of their staff
  • General staff members should report issues to their Manager or Supervisor, who will then create the appropriate ticket

Request vs. Problem Tickets

There are two types of tickets, and they follow different approval workflows:

Type Workflow Support Staff Action
Request Requires General Manager approval before appearing in your assigned tickets Will only see approved requests; proceed with work as assigned
Problem Goes directly to Support Staff without approval gate Appears immediately, but may still require approval before resolution

Support Staff Responsibilities

Approval Requirement: Even when a ticket appears in your queue, you must ensure that work is approved by Admin Staff (General Managers) before proceeding with resolution. When in doubt, verify approval before taking action.
  • Review assigned tickets promptly
  • Verify approval status before beginning work on any ticket
  • Document all work performed via comments
  • Update ticket status appropriately as work progresses
  • Escalate to management if clarification is needed

Project & Purchase Approvals

For Managers/Supervisors: Approvals for new projects and purchases must go through General Managers. Use the Request category when creating tickets for these itemsâ€”the built-in approval workflow will route them to GMs for review.

When submitting project or purchase requests, include:

  • Specific and detailed description of what is needed
  • Images or attachments (reference materials, screenshots, product links)
  • Estimated costs or budget requirements
  • Business justification or reason for the request

See Section 7: Creating New Tickets (pg. 7) for detailed instructions.

1. Signing In

Access the Help Desk system using your SkyPark Microsoft account credentials.

Preferred Method: Microsoft Teams

  1. Open Microsoft Teams
  2. Look for the Help Desk app icon in the left sidebar
  3. Click the icon to open the Help Desk within Teams
  4. You'll be automatically signed in with your Teams credentials
Recommended: Using the Teams app is the preferred way to access the Help Desk as it integrates with your existing workflow and keeps you signed in automatically.

Alternative: Web Browser Access

  1. Open your web browser and navigate to https://tickets.spsvent.net
  2. Click the "Sign in with Microsoft" button
  3. Enter your SkyPark email address and password when prompted
  4. If prompted, complete any multi-factor authentication (MFA) steps
  5. You will be redirected to the main dashboard
Tip: If you're already signed into other Microsoft 365 applications, you may be signed in automatically.

2. Understanding the Dashboard

After signing in, you'll see the main dashboard with the following areas:

Header Bar

Element Description
+ New button Create a new support ticket
Help link Access the online help documentation
Your Name Shows your logged-in account name
Support badge Indicates your role (Support Staff)
Sign out Log out of the Help Desk

Left Sidebar - Ticket List

  • Search bar - Search tickets by title, number, or content
  • View tabs - Active Tickets, By Priority, All Tickets, Open Only
  • Sort dropdown - Change how tickets are ordered
  • Filters button - Open advanced filtering options
  • Ticket list - Scrollable list of tickets you have access to view

Main Content Area

Shows the selected ticket's details, conversation history, and comment input area.

3. Viewing Tickets

As a Support Staff member, you can view ALL tickets in the system, regardless of department. However, your ability to edit tickets depends on your department assignment:

  • View: You can see all tickets across all departments
  • Edit: You can only modify tickets in your assigned department(s)
  • Comment: You can add comments to any ticket you can view
Tip: Tickets you cannot edit will show a "Read only" badge in the header.

Ticket List Information

Each ticket in the list shows:

  • Title - Brief description of the issue
  • Status badge - Current status (New, In Progress, etc.)
  • Approval badge - Shows if approved/pending (for Requests)
  • Department - Which team handles this ticket (Tech, Operations, etc.)
  • Requester name - Who submitted the ticket
  • Date/Time - When the ticket was created or last updated

Selecting a Ticket

  1. Click on any ticket in the left sidebar
  2. The ticket details will appear in the main content area
  3. The selected ticket will be highlighted with a green left border
Tip: Tickets marked "Your ticket" are ones you submitted. Tickets marked "Read only" can be viewed but not modified by you.

4. Filtering & Searching Tickets

Quick Search

Use the search bar at the top of the ticket list to quickly find tickets:

  • Search by ticket title keywords
  • Search by ticket number (e.g., "#234")
  • Search by requester name

View Tabs

Tab Shows
Active Tickets All non-closed tickets (default view)
By Priority Tickets grouped by priority level
All Tickets All tickets including closed ones
Open Only Only New and In Progress tickets

Advanced Filters

Click the "Filters" button to access advanced filtering options:

  • Status - Filter by New, In Progress, On Hold, Resolved, Closed
  • Priority - Filter by Urgent, High, Normal, Low
  • Department - Filter by Tech, Operations, Marketing, etc.
  • Category - Filter by Request or Problem
  • Assignee - Filter by who the ticket is assigned to
  • Location - Filter by park location
  • Date Range - Filter by when tickets were created
Tip: Since you can see all tickets, use filters to focus on what matters:
  • Filter by Department to see only your team's tickets
  • Filter by Assignee to see tickets assigned to specific people
  • Use Status filters to focus on open/active tickets

5. Working with Ticket Details

When you select a ticket, the detail view shows comprehensive information.

Ticket Header

  • Title - The ticket subject
  • Ticket Number - Unique identifier (e.g., #234)
  • Status Badge - Current status
  • Approval Badge - For Requests: Pending Approval or Approved
  • Category Path - Department / Sub-Category / Specific Type
  • Priority - Normal, High, or Urgent

Conversation Thread

The main area shows the full conversation history:

  • Description (green background) - Original ticket description
  • Comments - All replies and updates
  • Internal Notes (marked "Internal") - Staff-only comments hidden from requesters
  • System Messages - Automatic notifications (assignments, approvals, etc.)

Details Panel (Right Side)

Field Description
Status Current ticket status (editable if assigned to you)
Priority Urgency level (editable if assigned to you)
Assignee Person or group responsible for the ticket
Requester Person who submitted the ticket
Category Request or Problem
Department Which team handles this ticket
Location Where the issue is occurring

Note: If you see "Read only" on a ticket, you can view it and add comments, but cannot change status, priority, or other fields. This applies to tickets outside your assigned department.

6. Adding Comments

Comments allow you to communicate about tickets with requesters and other staff.

Adding a Public Comment

  1. Select the ticket you want to comment on
  2. Scroll to the comment input area at the bottom of the conversation
  3. Type your message in the "Add a comment..." text box
  4. Click the "Post Comment" button
Tip: Public comments are visible to the requester and will send them an email notification.

Adding an Internal Note

Internal notes are only visible to support staff and managers - the requester cannot see them.

  1. Select the ticket you want to comment on
  2. Check the box labeled "Internal note (hidden from requester - only managers & assigned staff can see)"
  3. Type your internal message
  4. Click the "Post Comment" button

Internal notes appear with a yellow "Internal" badge and are useful for:

  • Sharing technical details with other staff
  • Documenting troubleshooting steps
  • Coordinating with team members
  • Recording sensitive information

7. Creating New Tickets

As a Support Staff member, you can create tickets for yourself or on behalf of others.

Creating a New Ticket

  1. Click the "+ New" button in the header
  2. Enter a clear, descriptive Title for the issue
  3. Provide detailed information in the Description field
  4. Select the Category:
    • Problem - Something is broken or not working
    • Request - Need something new, changed, or set up
  5. Choose the appropriate Department (Tech, Operations, etc.)
  6. Select a Sub-Category that best matches the issue
  7. Optionally select a Specific Type if available
  8. Set the Priority level:
    • Low - Nice to have, no rush
    • Normal - Standard priority (default)
    • High - Important, needs prompt attention
    • Urgent - Critical business impact, drop everything
  9. Select the Location where the issue is occurring
  10. Click "Submit Ticket"
Important: Tickets categorized as "Request" require General Manager approval before support staff can work on them. You'll be notified when it's approved.

Project & Purchase Requests

When creating tickets for new projects or purchases that require approval, be thorough in your initial submission:

Include the following in your description:
  • Specific details - Exactly what is needed and why
  • Images/attachments - Photos, screenshots, product links, or reference materials
  • Estimated costs - Budget requirements, quotes, or price ranges
  • Business justification - How this benefits operations or addresses a need
  • Timeline - When this is needed by, if applicable

Providing complete information upfront helps General Managers make informed approval decisions and reduces back-and-forth delays.

Auto-Assignment Preview

Before submitting, the form shows who will be notified based on your category selections. This helps ensure the right people see your ticket.
